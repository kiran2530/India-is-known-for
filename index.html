INDIA

India is a large and important country located in South East Asia. In addition to its size, it is also the second most populated country in the World. India is famous for its colorful culture, flavorful cuisine, and deep traditions.The country shares borders with several notable countries such as China, Nepal, and Pakistan. It also borders the east part of the Arabian Sea and is south of the Indian Ocean. There are many interesting things in the country that make it uniquely India.


WHAT IS INDIA FAMOUS FOR?

India is known for its high-energy celebrations, incredible landmarks, and rich history. These are reflected by several monuments constructed in the most famous cities. Many of these structures are religious, such as The Taj Mahal and Jama Masjid. India is also famous for their exotic coasts, especially Goa, where there are long beaches and delicate nature.


* LANDMARKS AND ARCHITECTURE

India is a country known for its rich architectural heritage and iconic landmarks. The architecture of India reflects its diverse cultural, historical, and religious influences, spanning thousands of years. Indian architecture is incredibly diverse, reflecting the country's rich history, diverse cultures, and various dynastic influences over millennia. like as below the top Eight Landmarks & Architecture in India.

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the right bank of the river Yamuna in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India. It was commissioned in 1631 by the fifth Mughal emperor, Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favourite wife, Mumtaz Mahal; it also houses the tomb of Shah Jahan himself.

Golden Temple

Harmandir Sahib, internationally known as the Golden Temple, is a place of worship located in Punjab. In addition to Buddhism, India is famous for its large population of Sikhs. The Golden Temple is a holy building and place of sanctuary for all believers of Sikhism known as a gurdwara.

More Info

Red Fort

The Red Fort, or Lal Qila, is a massive fort built in the 17th century by Shah Jahan. It was constructed as the palace fort for Shahjahanabad, now known as Old Delhi. This is one of the most significant and famous cities of India at the time, as all governing activities took place there.

More Info

Gateway Of India

The Gateway of India is a relatively new building constructed in Mumbai. It is a stone arch overlooking the Arabian Sea behind it.The purpose of the construction of this monument was to celebrate King George V. King George V was the first monarch of England to visit India, along with Queen Mary, in 1911.

More Info

Hawa Mahal

The Hawa Mahal is a beautiful palace located in Jaipur, one of the largest and most famous cities of India. The Hawa Mahal is unique because of its pink and red sandstone structure, making it easily visible and standing out from the surrounding buildings. It is famous for its windows.

More Info

Jama Masjid

India is famous for its variety of mosques. A well-known mosque is the Jama Masjid, found in New Delhi.The Jama Masjid is a beautiful structure made of red sandstone and marble. It is a perfect example of Mughal architecture, as it was built in the 17th century when this was the most prominent style.

More Info

Kerala Backwaters

The Kerala Backwaters are one of the most scenic natural landmarks of India. Although India is famous for its historical monuments and architecture, they also have many picturesque water bodies and phenomena. Essentially, the Kerala Backwaters are a series of lakes parallel to the Arabian Sea.

More Info

Qutb Minar

Qutub Minar is the second tallest and ever attractive historical monument of India located in Delhi at Aurobindo Marg, Mehrauli. It is built in unique architectural style using red sandstones and marble. It is considered that Mughals built this victory tower to celebrate their victories over the Rajputs.

* FOOD

Indian dishes strike a delicate balance between sweet and spicy; therefore, tempting many palates and becoming more and more popular for dine-in and take-out cuisine. Whether you prefer vegetarian, meat-based meals, your taste runs to mild fare, or you crave dishes with some heat, there is almost always an Indian dish to please your taste buds.Here are some of the most popular dishes to try:

Masala

Masala is a blend of spices that can be used in a variety of dishes which India is famous for. Although it is not one particular dish itself, it can be incorporated into appetizers, mains, desserts, and even teas.Usually, masala culminates in five simple spices: cardamon, cumin, coriander, cinnamon, and cloves. Other blends can include nutmeg, fennel seeds, and pepper.

More Info

Biryani

Biryani is a mixed rice and chicken dish popular in South Asian countries. Made from its original recipe it is a relatively mild dish, however, India is famous for using spices to kick it up a notch. The two main ingredients in biryani are chicken rice. Spices are what give this meal its characteristic taste and smell, of which there are many.

More Info

Dosa

Dosa is a thin, savory pancake that is enjoyed mostly in the southern parts of India. Usually, it is eaten with a zesty filling. A dosa itself is made up of lentils and rice which are fermented and fried. It comes in a variety of shapes, either rolled like a wrap or split into a half-moon shape. A masala dosa, for example, is filled with a spicy potato mixture.

More Info

Curry

Curry is a broad term for a dish that is made up of a flavourful sauce with meat, usually plated with rice. The main thing which makes Indian curries distinct is their creamy consistency, and use of chicken over beef.The most popular curry which India is famous for is Goan, made of fish, and Dhansak, made of lamb.

More Info

Tandoori

Tandoori originates from ‘tandoor’ a type of clay oven fired by charcoal or wood. As a result, tandoori involves cooking meat in this oven. Popular tandoori meat usually includes chicken, served with pilau rice and naan bread. India is famous for its spices, and tandoori is no different. A typical tandoori spice is savory and can be adapted to taste spicier.

More Info

Gulab Jamun

In addition to popular main dishes, India is famous for an array of desserts. The most known of these is gulab jamun. The name translates to rose and berry, which is a reference to the syrup they are coated in and their circular shape. Gulab jamun is a sweet treat made mostly of milk, flour, and butter. The mixture is rolled and fried.

More Info

Samosa

This delicious option is a food that even those new to Indian cooking may have tried. As a fried dough pocket filled with delectable ingredients such as potato, onion, spices, and peas, samosas are often eaten as snacks in India. They are especially delicious when topped with sauces or chutneys.

More Info

Pav Bhaji

Pav Bhaji is an Indian fast food consisting of a thick spicy vegetable gravy served with soft dinner rolls. In Hindi and Marathi, the word 'Pav' means 'Bread roll or dinner roll' and 'Bhaji' means 'a vegetable dish'. Since these are served together as a meal, it is known as Pav Bhaji.

More Info

* CITIES AND TRAVEL DESTINATIONS

India is the seventh biggest country in the world. It has numerous languages, ethnic groups, cultures, monuments, palaces, backwaters, waterfalls and other spectaculars. There is no such place in India that can be tagged as a boring destination for tourism. Here are some of the most popular Cities to travel and enjoy.

Mumbai

Mumbai is one of the most famous cities of India, as it is the largest and has the largest population of inhabitants of over 22 million. Due to its sheer size, Mumbai is well-known for being fast-paced and dynamic. It is rich in cultural aspects such as art, nightlife, and theatre. Additionally, Indian street food is highly-acclaimed and cheap without compromising on flavors.

More Info

New Delhi

New Delhi is known for being the capital city of India; but one of the lesser-known facts about Delhi is that it hasn’t always held that status. Before the city became the capital in 1911, Kolkata was the hub of governance. New Delhi is often recognized for housing the government of India as well as the supreme court.

More Info

Kolkata

In terms of history, India is famous for shifting its capital from Kolkata to New Delhi under British rule in 1911. Prior to this, Kolkata was the capital for almost 140 years, therefore making it one of the most famous cities of India.Kolkata earnt its nickname ‘The City of Joy’ because of the festivity with is always present.

More Info

Goa

Goa is famous for being one of the most peaceful and relaxing cities in India. It is ideal for family vacations, solo trips, or even an adventurous place to visit with friends.The long coastline of Goa makes it perfect for beach holidays. It is located on the southwest coast of India along the Arabian Sea, allowing for many resorts to be built.

More Info

Bangalore

Bangalore, called Bengaluru by the locals, is a large and modern city in India. It is often referred to as the ‘Silicon Valley’ of India due to its extensive use of information technology. This makes it an important research institution and software hub. In contrast to its modernized reputation, there are many religious sites in Bangalore too.

More Info

Agra

Agra is notable for the most significant landmark in India – the famous Taj Mahal. This is the mausoleum housing the remains of Mumtaz Mahal, which is a must-see Indian attraction. This is not only for religious purposes but also to marvel at the stunning architecture.Agra Fort is a 15th-century fortification where several emperors resided during this period.

More Info

Kochi

Kochi, in the southern state of Kerala, is a breath of fresh air. Situated on the coast, the city has a distinctly different feel compared to any more northern metropolis. The port town has been packed full of cultural diversity since it began trading with Arabs, Chinese and Europeans.

More Info

Mysore

Mysore palace was built out of wood in 1897 and still stands today as an icon of intricate craftsmanship.As night falls, the palace is illuminated to create a magical golden glow. The city isn’t all about its multiple monuments; the beautiful Brindavan Gardens, along with the Karanji Lake, are both special spots in the city to spend time reflecting after a trip to the bustling city bazaars.

* HISTORY, CULTURE, & TRADITIONS

India is a diverse and culturally rich country with a long and complex history. Its history, culture, and traditions have evolved over thousands of years, and they continue to play a significant role in shaping the country's identity. Here's an overview of India's history, culture, and traditions :

Nag Panchami

India is famous for its variety of festivals, in which there is usually a god, person or animal to be worshipped. In Nag Panchami, people worship snakes. Every year during the summer season, candy and flowers are offered to the serpent. This falls on the fifth day of ‘Shravan’, which is the month of July or August in the Hindu calendar.

More Info

Yoga

Although yoga is a widespread sport that is practiced globally, it originated in India over five thousand years ago. The father of yoga, known as Maharishi Patanjali, was one of the first to introduce it. Yoga involves the practice of patience and discipline whilst maintaining numerous positions. Famous poses include the lotus, downward dog, and crane.

More Info

Vedic Chant

India is famous for its devotion to Hinduism, which is the principal religion and has been since 1500 BC. As a result, much of the culture and traditions which are practiced can be traced back to religion. Vedic chanting is one of the oldest vocal traditions, which comes from Vedic texts. Essentially, these are pieces of literature concerning hymns and protection spells.

More Info

Kumbh Mela

Kumbh Mela is a huge pilgrimage in which the population participates. There are four sites: Allahabad, Haridwar, Nashik, and Ujjain. All of these are famous cities of India. This pilgrimage is held for over 12 years and is celebrated every 4 years. During the pilgrimage, which can last up to one month, people must bathe in the river which is considered to be sacred.

More Info

Diwali

Diwali, also known as the Festival of Lights, is a popular gathering held annually. Whilst it is primarily a Hindu festival held in India, it can be celebrated by all cultures. Many people visit annually, just to watch the beautiful fireworks and intricately designed lamps. Over five days, a series of rituals occur. The aim of these is to allow prosperity and good fortune to wash over us.

More Info

Hola Mohalla

The Hola Mohalla is a huge festival which India is famous for. In addition to Hinduism, much of the country practices Sikhism. Hence, this festival celebrates a noble Sikh warrior called Nihang. For three days, several military practices are held. Although there are many curated battles on display for audiences, there are also other exhibitions.

More Info

Traditional Cloth

Traditional clothing in India is loud and colorful. And also, known for proudly wearing vibrant saris, lehengas, and even bridalwear. On the other hand, a lehenga is a more formal piece of clothing. It is a skirt that is embroidered and jeweled, worn for weddings and festivals.

More Info

Hinduism

Hinduism is one of the largest religions worldwide. In India, it is the predominant religion, with over three-quarters of the country practicing it. Therefore, many aspects of everyday culture are influenced by Hinduism and its beliefs.In India, there are several popular festivals during the year. Holi, Diwali and Navratri are all religious holidays practiced by Hindus.
